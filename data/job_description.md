**Job Description: Senior AI Engineer — Founding Team**
**Company:** Redrob AI (Series A AI-native talent intelligence platform)
**Location:** Pune/Noida, India (Hybrid — flexible cadence) | Open to relocation candidates from Tier-1 Indian cities
**Employment Type:** Full-time
**Experience Required:** 5–9 years (see "what we mean by this" below)

**Let's be honest about this role**
We're going to write this JD differently from most. We're a Series A company that just raised our round and we're building a new AI Engineering org from scratch. Instead of pretending we have a fixed checklist, we're going to tell you what we actually need.

If you've spent your career at Google or Meta and you want a well-scoped role with a defined ladder, this isn't it. If you've spent your career bouncing between early-stage startups and you want to "just code" without thinking about product or recruiter workflows or eval frameworks, this also isn't it.

We need someone comfortable with two things that sound contradictory: deep technical depth in modern ML systems (embeddings, retrieval, ranking, LLMs, fine-tuning) AND a scrappy product-engineering attitude (willing to ship a working ranker in a week even if "obviously suboptimal"). We'd rather you tilt slightly toward shipper than toward researcher.

**What you'd actually be doing**
Own the intelligence layer of Redrob's product — ranking, retrieval, and matching systems. First 90 days: audit the current BM25 + rule-based system and find the highest-leverage fixes (weeks 1-3); ship a v2 ranking system with embeddings, hybrid retrieval, and probably LLM-based re-ranking (weeks 4-8); build offline/online eval infrastructure (weeks 9-12). Beyond that: long-term architecture, mentoring (team growing 4→12 engineers), working with the recruiter-experience PM.

**What we mean by "5-9 years"**
A range, not a requirement. We'll consider candidates outside the band if other signals are strong. **The actual disqualifiers:**
- Pure research environments (academic labs, research-only roles) with no production deployment — will not move forward.
- "AI experience" consisting primarily of recent (under 12 months) LangChain-calls-OpenAI projects, without substantial pre-LLM-era ML production experience — will probably not move forward.
- Senior engineers who haven't written production code in 18+ months due to moving into pure architecture/tech-lead roles — will probably not move forward. This role writes code.

**Things you absolutely need**
- Production experience with embeddings-based retrieval systems (sentence-transformers, OpenAI embeddings, BGE, E5, or similar) deployed to real users — specific model doesn't matter, operational experience with drift/index refresh/retrieval-quality regression does.
- Production experience with vector databases or hybrid search infra (Pinecone, Weaviate, Qdrant, Milvus, OpenSearch, Elasticsearch, FAISS, or similar).
- Strong Python, real code quality.
- Hands-on experience designing evaluation frameworks for ranking systems (NDCG, MRR, MAP, offline-to-online correlation, A/B interpretation).

**Nice to have, not required:** LLM fine-tuning (LoRA/QLoRA/PEFT), learning-to-rank models, HR-tech background, distributed systems/large-scale inference experience, open-source contributions.

**Things we explicitly do NOT want**
- **Title-chasers**: career trajectory optimizing Senior→Staff→Principal by switching companies every ~1.5 years. We need someone planning to stay 3+ years.
- **Framework enthusiasts**: GitHub full of LangChain tutorials, blog posts titled "How I used [hot framework] to build [demo]" — fine as a person, not what we need. We need systems thinkers, not framework tourists.
- **People who have only worked at consulting firms** (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Accenture, Cognizant, Capgemini, etc.) their entire career. If currently at one of these but with prior product-company experience, that's fine.
- **People whose primary expertise is computer vision, speech, or robotics** without significant NLP/IR exposure.
- **People whose work has been entirely closed-source/proprietary for 5+ years** with zero external validation (papers, talks, open-source).

**Location, comp, logistics**
Pune/Noida preferred but flexible; Hyderabad, Pune, Mumbai, Delhi NCR candidates welcome. Outside India: case-by-case, no visa sponsorship. Notice period: sub-30-day strongly preferred (can buy out up to 30 days); 30+ day candidates still in scope but bar is higher.

**How to read between the lines — the ideal candidate is roughly:**
- 6-8 years total experience, of which 4-5 years in applied ML/AI roles at **product companies** (not pure services).
- Has shipped at least one end-to-end ranking, search, or recommendation system to real users at meaningful scale.
- Has strong, defensible opinions about retrieval (hybrid vs dense), evaluation (offline vs online), and LLM integration (fine-tune vs prompt).
- Located in or willing to relocate to Noida or Pune.
- Active on the Redrob platform / clear signal of being in the job market.
- **We are aware this is a narrow profile. We are not expecting to find many matches in a 100K candidate pool — we'd rather see 10 great matches than 1000 maybes.**

**Final note for hackathon participants:**
The "right answer" is NOT "find candidates whose skills section contains the most AI keywords" — that's an explicitly built-in trap. The right answer reasons about the gap between what the JD says and what it means. A Tier 5 candidate who never writes "RAG" or "Pinecone" but whose career history shows they built a recommendation system at a product company is a fit. A candidate with every AI keyword listed but the title "Marketing Manager" is not, no matter how perfect the skill list looks. Also weigh behavioral signals — a perfect-on-paper candidate who hasn't logged in for 6 months with a 5% recruiter response rate is, for hiring purposes, not actually available. Down-weight accordingly.
